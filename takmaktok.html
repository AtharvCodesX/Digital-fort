The Execution Point of Raigad Fort

üìú Historical Background

Takmak Tok was the dreaded execution point of Raigad Fort. During the reign of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, enemies, traitors, and criminals were punished here by being thrown into the deep valley below. It symbolized justice and discipline in Swarajya.

üåÑ Location & Structure

Situated on the western edge of Raigad Fort, Takmak Tok rises more than 1200 meters above sea level. The cliff drops almost 1000 feet straight down, making it both terrifying and breathtaking. Today, railings have been installed for tourist safety.

‚öîÔ∏è Importance in Swarajya

Takmak Tok served as a reminder of the strict justice system under Shivaji Maharaj. Public punishments here ensured that loyalty and discipline were maintained within the fort and across the empire.

üå∏ Present Day

Today, Takmak Tok is a tourist attraction and viewpoint. From here, visitors can see spectacular views of the Konkan valleys. While the scenery is mesmerizing, the place still carries the chilling stories of its past.

‚ú® Interesting Facts

  • "Takmak" means punishment and "Tok" means cliff in Marathi.
  • Executions here were public, to instill fear and respect for law.
  • Today, it is one of the most visited spots on Raigad Fort for both history lovers and trekkers.
