ЁЯУЬ Historical Importance

The Samadhi of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj at Raigad Fort is the final resting place of the great Maratha king. After his death in 1680, he was cremated here, and this sacred site became his eternal memorial.

ЁЯП░ Architecture

The Samadhi is a black stone structure covered with saffron cloth and flowers by devotees. A magnificent statue of Shivaji Maharaj seated on his throne stands nearby, facing the fort.

ЁЯжБ Life of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj

Born: 19 February 1630, Shivneri Fort
Parents: Shahaji Bhosale and Jijabai

From a young age, Shivaji Maharaj was inspired by his mother Jijabai and his teacher Dadoji Konddev. At age 16, he captured Torna Fort and began building the Maratha Swarajya.

ЁЯМ╕ Cultural Significance

The Samadhi is visited by thousands of people every year to pay their respects. Festivals like Shiv Jayanti and Punyatithi are celebrated here with great devotion. It stands as a symbol of Maratha valor, sacrifice, and Swarajya.

ЁЯМЯ Legacy

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj passed away on 3 April 1680 at Raigad. His Samadhi here continues to inspire millions. His vision of Swarajya laid the foundation for the Maratha Empire and later India's freedom struggle.
